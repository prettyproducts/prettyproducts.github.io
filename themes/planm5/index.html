The finance function

The finance function within an organization

Performance

Financial Planning and Analysis

STRATEGIC PLANNING

This area focuses on creating budgets, forecasts, and financial models to project the company's financial performance. FP&A professionals analyze historical data, market trends, and business strategies to provide insights that guide decision-making and strategic planning.

Flows

Treasury Management

ORGANIZATION

Treasury management involves managing the company's cash flows, liquidity, and financial assets. Treasury professionals are responsible for optimizing cash management, overseeing banking relationships, and ensuring that the organization has adequate funds to meet its operational and strategic needs.

Investments

Capital Budgeting and Investment Decisions

CASH MANAGEMENT

Finance professionals evaluate potential investments and allocate capital to projects or initiatives that are expected to generate returns and add value to the company. This involves analyzing the costs, benefits, risks, and potential outcomes of various investment opportunities.

Protect

Risk Management

INSURANCE

Managing financial risks is crucial to protect the company's assets and interests. This area involves identifying, assessing, and mitigating various types of risks, such as market risk, credit risk, operational risk, and more. Techniques like hedging, insurance, and diversification are used to manage risk exposure.

Reporting

Financial Reporting and Accounting

ACCOUNTABILITY

The finance function is responsible for accurate and timely financial reporting, including preparing financial statements (such as the balance sheet, income statement, and cash flow statement) in compliance with relevant accounting standards (e.g., GAAP or IFRS).

Types of reports

  • Investment Performance Reports
  • Portfolio Summary Reports
  • Financial Planning Reports
  • Risk Assessment Reports
  • Tax Efficiency Reports

These reports detail the performance of the client's investment portfolio over a specific period. They may include comparisons to benchmark indices and provide insights into how well the investments are performing.

Portfolio summary reports offer an overview of the client's investment holdings, including asset allocation, diversification, and the percentage of investments in different asset classes (e.g., stocks, bonds, real estate).

These reports outline the client's overall financial plan, including goals, strategies, and recommendations for achieving those goals. They can cover topics like retirement planning, education funding, estate planning, and more.

Risk assessment reports help clients understand their risk tolerance and the level of risk in their investment portfolio. Advisors may use tools to quantify risk and ensure that the client's investments align with their risk tolerance.

Tax efficiency reports focus on optimizing the client's investments to minimize tax liabilities. They might suggest strategies like tax-loss harvesting and selecting tax-advantaged investment accounts.
